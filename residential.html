We are passionate about good buildings and the spaces in between. But we know that unless our internal and external team of experts truly engage with design, these buildings and spaces become inert, unloved and engage no one.

For places to be successful they need to be attractive, safe and resilient; they need to stand the test of time and they should flourish as they age.

High quality architecture, urban design and place making has the opportunity to change society. It is essential that we as designers and developers get this right.

It is only when buildings and spaces respond to people's needs that they can truly be called a 'place'. And this is what we really care about creating — great places.

To create a successful 'place' we use well established principles to build up layers of design decisions, each having a positive impact on the next, and all creating a composition which is pleasing, attractive, safe and loved.

How We Work

Every residential project, whatever its scale, moves through the same disciplined sequence — from first conversation to the day you move in.

01

Brief & Feasibility

Understanding the site, the brief and the planning context before a single line is drawn.

02

Concept Design

Exploring layout, scale and character through sketches, models and early 3D visualisation.

03

Planning

Preparing and submitting applications, working closely with local planning authorities across Yorkshire.

04

Technical Design

Developing the scheme into a fully coordinated, buildable model using our BIM platform.

05

Construction Support

Remaining on hand through to completion, resolving detail on site as the building takes shape.

Common Questions

A few things people usually ask before getting in touch about a new build or extension.

Do you design both new build homes and extensions?

Yes. We work across the full range of residential projects, from single-storey extensions and renovations to entire new build homes and multi-plot developments.

Do I need planning permission for my project?

Most new build homes and many extensions require planning permission, though some smaller works may fall under permitted development rights. We can advise on the right route for your site as part of our initial feasibility work.

How long does the design and planning process take?

Every project differs depending on scale and the local planning authority, but our process generally runs from feasibility and concept design through to a planning submission, followed by technical design and construction support — see "How We Work" above for the full sequence.

Which areas do you cover?

We're based at Dean Clough Mills in Halifax and work across Yorkshire and the wider North of England, including Leeds, Bradford, Harrogate, Sheffield, Wakefield and Huddersfield.
